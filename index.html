About Me

I am a PhD candidate specializing in control systems, robotics, and optimization. My research focuses on resilient estimator-based control methods, multi-agent path planning, and learning-based control strategies for dynamical systems, particularly in the presence of disturbances and noise. I have a deep interest in bridging the gap between low-frequency planners and high-frequency controllers, working on the integration of continuous-time control updates with B-splines for real-time applications.

Research Interests

  • Resilient Estimator-based Control Barrier Functions for Dynamical Systems
  • Learning-based Control with Multi-agent Reinforcement Learning
  • Path Planning and Safe Control Strategies for Multi-robot Systems
  • Optimization-based Planners for Continuous-Time Control in Robotics
  • Embedded Systems and Digital Control for Mechatronics Applications
